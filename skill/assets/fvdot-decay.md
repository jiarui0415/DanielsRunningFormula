# VDOT Decay During Training Breaks (Table 9.1)

Source: Jack Daniels' Running Formula (4th ed., 2022), Chapter 9, Table 9.1

## How to use

When a runner's most recent race time is more than 5 days old:
1. Count days since last hard training or race
2. Choose FVDOT-1 (no leg exercise during break) or FVDOT-2 (did cross-training with leg involvement)
3. Multiply current VDOT × fraction = adjusted training VDOT

**Example:** Runner's race was 28 days ago, did no cross-training.
VDOT 52 × 0.931 = adjusted VDOT 48.4 → use VDOT 48 for training paces.

## Decay Table

| Training break | FVDOT-1 (no leg training) | FVDOT-2 (with leg cross-training) |
|----------------|--------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Up to 5 days   | 1.000 | 1.000 |
| 6 days         | 0.997 | 0.998 |
| 7 days         | 0.994 | 0.997 |
| 10 days        | 0.985 | 0.992 |
| 14 days        | 0.973 | 0.986 |
| 21 days        | 0.952 | 0.976 |
| 28 days        | 0.931 | 0.965 |
| 35 days        | 0.910 | 0.955 |
| 42 days        | 0.889 | 0.944 |
| 49 days        | 0.868 | 0.934 |
| 56 days        | 0.847 | 0.923 |
| 63 days        | 0.826 | 0.913 |
| 70 days        | 0.805 | 0.902 |
| 72+ days       | 0.800 | 0.900 |

**Floor:** After ~10 weeks, VDOT stabilizes at ~80% (no cross-training) or ~90% (with cross-training). Weight gain during the break can reduce VDOT further.

---

## Cross-training qualifies as FVDOT-2 if it involves legs

- Deep-water running ✓
- Elliptical trainer ✓
- Cycling ✓
- Uphill treadmill walking ✓
- Swimming only: use FVDOT-1 (arms, not legs)

## Return-to-training mileage ramp (Table 9.2)

| Break length | Return protocol |
|-------------|----------------|
| ≤5 days | Resume at 100% of previous load |
| 6–28 days | First half at 50% load, second half at 75% load |
| 4–8 weeks | First third at 33%, second third at 50%, final third at 75% |
| 8+ weeks | Week 1–3: 33% (max 30 mi/wk) → Week 4–6: 50% (max 40 mi/wk) → Week 7–9: 70% + strides (max 60 mi/wk) → Week 10–12: 85% + strides + R → Week 13+: 100% + T work |
