# Jack Daniels' No-Equipment Strength Circuit

Source: Jack Daniels' Running Formula (4th ed., 2022), Chapter 9

## Overview

10-station circuit, no equipment required. Full body workout with running recovery stations built in. Go through all 10 stations **3 times per session**.

Before starting: establish your 1-minute push-up max and 1-minute crunch max (you need these numbers for stations 1 and 4).

---

## The Circuit

**Station 1 — Push-Ups**
- Reps: half of your 1-minute maximum (e.g., if you can do 30 push-ups in 1 min, do 15)
- Form: plank position, arms straight, lower chest to near-floor, push back up

**Station 2 — Side Leg Lifts**
- Reps: 10 per leg (20 total)
- Form: lie on side, support on forearm, raise top leg to shoulder height, return

**Station 3 — High Knees**
- Reps: 30 per leg (60 total), running in place
- Form: standing, alternate lifting each knee as high as possible, continuous motion

**Station 4 — Crunches**
- Reps: half of your 1-minute maximum
- Form: lie on back, knees bent, feet on floor, hands behind head (not pulling), raise head and shoulders to upright

**Station 5 — Recovery Run** *(not a resistance station)*
- Duration: 1 minute or 400 meters at easy pace
- Purpose: allow recovery before next resistance station

**Station 6 — Stretching** *(not a resistance station)*
- Duration: 2 minutes, any stretches you like

**Station 7 — Squat Thrusts (Burpees)**
- Reps: 10
- Form: standing → squat with hands on ground → jump legs back to push-up position → jump back to squat → stand. One rep.

**Station 8 — Leg Lifts**
- Reps: 10 per leg per direction (40 total)
- First: rest on forearms facing up, hips off ground, one leg bent for balance, lift other leg to height of bent leg. 10 per leg.
- Then: flip over, rest on forearms facing down, keep one leg straight, raise other leg up as far as comfortable. 10 per leg.

**Station 9 — Arm and Leg Flapping**
- Reps: 20 total
- Form: lie on belly, arms over head, legs straight. Alternately flap arms and legs up and down until each has been raised and lowered 20 times.

**Station 10 — Recovery Run** *(not a resistance station)*
- Duration: 2 minutes or 800 meters at easy pace
- Purpose: allow full recovery before repeating circuit

## Prescription by Training Phase

| Phase | Frequency | Timing |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Phase I | 2–3× per week | After easy runs |
| Phase II | 2× per week | After easy runs |
| Phase III | 2× per week | After easy runs |
| Phase IV | 1× per week | After easy run |
| Taper (final 2 weeks) | Drop entirely | — |

**Progression rule:** Stay at current load for 4 weeks before increasing. When you do increase, add one round (go from 3 rounds to 4) rather than adding stations.

**Tracking progress:** Time yourself through all 3 rounds every few weeks. You'll notice significant improvement. Stations 5 and 10 are recovery runs — keep them genuinely easy.
